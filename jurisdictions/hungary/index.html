Executive Summary

Corporate secretarial in Hungary is the practical governance and compliance function through which companies maintain their formal existence, statutory records and visibility in the Hungarian company registration system. It operates through coordination of corporate decisions, maintenance of records and systematic use of the Cégjegyzék, courts of registration, the Company Information Service and official reporting and gazette systems.

The Hungarian company register is public and basic information about registered companies is accessible free of charge through the official electronic company register at e-cegjegyzek.hu. The data of companies registered at any Hungarian court of registration are available free of charge through the Service of Company Information and Electronic Company Registration of the Ministry of Justice.

Company information can be searched using company name, corporate registration number or tax number. Accessing further content, authentic copies and additional corporate documents is subject to charges or administrative procedures through the relevant official channels.

The Hungarian registry environment is complemented by public access to annual financial statements through the official e-beszámoló reporting system and by company notices published through the company gazette environment. Together these systems create a multi-source compliance landscape in which company status, reporting and official announcements must be managed coherently.

Corporate governance in Hungary is also supported by the Corporate Governance Recommendations published by the Budapest Stock Exchange. These recommendations are intended to help public limited companies align with internationally recognised standards of responsible corporate governance and improve transparency around management and operations.
