Executive Summary

Corporate secretarial in Portugal is the practical governance and compliance function through which companies maintain their formal existence, statutory records and visibility in the commercial registry system. It operates through coordination of corporate decisions, maintenance of records and systematic use of Registo Comercial, IRN portals, digital certificates and corporate governance codes.

In Portugal the business register is governed by the Business Register Code adopted by Decree-Law No 403/86 of 3 December 1986 and commercial registry services enable access to acts and facts regarding the legal status of companies, partnerships and other entities or sole traders. Registration requests can be submitted at business registry offices, by mail or online via ePortugal’s Empresa Online service, which supports incorporation and commercial registration using digital credentials and IRN systems.

The Registo Comercial and IRN environment includes Empresa Online and Empresa na Hora pathways for company formation. Empresa Online allows digital incorporation using the Citizen Card or Digital Mobile Key and supports commercial registry requests including certificates, while Empresa na Hora facilitates in-person incorporation within very short timelines at designated desks, with subsequent registrations and compliance steps.

The Certidão Permanente is a key digital document in Portuguese corporate secretarial practice. It is a commercial registry certificate issued by the Instituto dos Registos e do Notariado that serves as definitive proof of a company’s legal status and is accessible 24/7 through a unique access code requested via the IRN portal by providing the company’s NIPC or name and paying the prescribed fee.

Company registration steps for foreign and domestic founders commonly involve obtaining Portuguese tax identification numbers (NIF), reserving company names through the Central Registry operated by IRN, depositing share capital and notarising incorporation deeds. Registration with the commercial registry leads to issuance of a Certidão Permanente confirming legal existence within typical online processing times of 5–10 days or approximately 60 minutes via Empresa na Hora desks.

Corporate governance in Portugal is primarily framed by the Portuguese Commercial Companies Code, which governs corporate bodies and shareholder general meetings, and by soft-law codes issued by the Portuguese Securities Market Commission (CMVM) and the Portuguese Corporate Governance Institute (IPCG). CMVM approved a new Corporate Governance Code in 2013, and Regulation 1/2010 made it mandatory for listed companies to implement CMVM’s Corporate Governance Code, while a private-initiative IPCG code also applies and a unified corporate governance code under IPCG is under development.

Process Flow

Process Flow describes how Portuguese corporate secretarial work typically proceeds from formation or trigger event to maintenance outcome.

1. Pre-Registration EssentialsFounders obtain Portuguese tax identification numbers (NIF) and, where non-resident, appoint Portuguese tax representatives; they prepare business rationale and documentation for registry and tax authorities.
2. Company Name ReservationSubmit one or more name proposals to Portugal’s Central Registry under IRN and receive a Certificado de Admissibilidade (Name Admissibility Certificate) within typical 24-hour timelines, with reservation validity for defined periods.
3. Articles of Association and CapitalDraft articles specifying registered address, business activities, share capital and governance structure and deposit share capital in provisional accounts, obtaining bank deposit certificates.
4. Incorporation and Commercial Registry EntryNotarise incorporation deeds online via Empresa Online or in person via Empresa na Hora, then submit deeds and supporting documents to Registo Comercial to obtain entry and Certidão Permanente.
5. Post-Registration ComplianceActivate tax and social security registrations, file beneficial ownership declarations and comply with reporting and accounting requirements, including submission of accounts to the commercial registry.
6. Ongoing MaintenanceUpdate commercial registry entries and Certidão Permanente data for structural changes and governance developments and adjust governance practices under CMVM and IPCG codes.
