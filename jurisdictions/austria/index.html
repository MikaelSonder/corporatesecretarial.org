Executive Summary

Corporate secretarial in Austria is the practical governance and compliance function through which companies maintain their formal existence, statutory records and Business Register entries in the Firmenbuch. It operates through coordination of corporate decisions, maintenance of records and the filing and purchase of extracts and documents from the official Business Register Database.

The Austrian commercial register (Handelsregister) has been replaced by the Firmenbuch, which is managed as an electronic database maintained by regional commercial courts and held centrally in electronic format at the Federal Computing Center. Anyone may access the commercial register database to obtain information on entries in the register, and basic summary information containing main details about a legal entity is available free of charge.

The Business Register Database is the official online database of the Federal Ministry of Justice and contains all companies registered in Austria. It enables online enquiries and provides authentic, legally binding information on legally relevant data; current extracts can be obtained instantly by entering the company name, Business Register Number (FN) or VAT Identification Number (UID), while deleted (historical) data can also be issued on request.

From the commercial register database it is possible to retrieve both register extracts and documents from the document archive, including articles of association and financial statements. Products subject to a charge require registration and online payment, while summary information can be accessed without further conditions; permanent online access with all search options can be obtained via a clearing centre arrangement.

GISA, the Austrian Business Licence Information System, contains important business-related data for all commercial operations established in Austria, with queries free of charge and requiring no registration. Extracts from GISA are available in German or English and are authentic public documents, which makes trade licence data an important layer of corporate secretarial information alongside Firmenbuch entries.

Corporate governance in Austria is shaped by legal provisions and the Austrian Code of Corporate Governance, which provides corporations with a framework for management and control of enterprises. The Code is applied voluntarily, and is seen as an important building block for the development and recovery of the Austrian capital market, with a working group responsible for its publication and updating.

Process Flow

Process Flow describes how Austrian corporate secretarial work typically proceeds from formation or trigger event to maintenance outcome.

1. Pre-IncorporationChoose legal form, define company name and prepare incorporation documents and capital structure.
2. Application PreparationCompile applications for entry in the Companies Register, signed by managing directors with certified signatures and including required information on company name, legal form, registered office, business address, partners, capital and managing directors.
3. Filing with Companies RegisterSubmit applications on paper or electronically via the Electronic Legal Transactions system, with required attachments such as notarised memorandum of association and proof of capital contributions.
4. Business Register EntryReceive entry in the Firmenbuch and Business Register Database and obtain registration number and official confirmation.
5. Trade Licence Registration in GISAFor trade activities, submit applications for trade licences and register operations in GISA, then obtain authentic extracts.
6. Governance and Records SetupEstablish internal records, governance calendars and procedures for recording decisions and changes.
7. Ongoing Updates and ExtractsFile changes in directors, addresses and capital, obtain current and historic extracts and documents and maintain alignment between records and registry data.
8. Governance Reporting (Where Applicable)For listed companies, apply Austrian Code of Corporate Governance and report on compliance and governance arrangements.
